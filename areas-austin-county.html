About the County

Rolling ranch country. Real Texas history. An hour from Houston.

Austin County is a small rural county directly west of Houston, named for Stephen F. Austin, the founder of the original Anglo-American colony in Texas. The county seat is Bellville. Sealy sits along Interstate 10 on the southern edge. Other small towns scatter across the county — Industry, Cat Spring, Wallis, San Felipe.

The land here is gently rolling, productive, and well-suited to cattle ranching, hay production, hunting, and row crops. Many ranches in the county have been in the same family for multiple generations, and the German and Czech heritage in towns like Industry and Cat Spring runs deep.

The county sits inside the Texas Triangle, the megaregion bounded by Houston, DFW, San Antonio, and Austin. Roughly an hour west of Houston via I-10. About an hour and a half to the city of Austin. About two hours from San Antonio. That central location is one of the reasons buyers from all three metros end up looking in Austin County for ranches, weekend properties, and family land.

Ag exemption is widely available across the county under cattle, hay, or wildlife management. J4LP works Austin County actively and routes leads through the central line to the right agent for your situation.

Common Questions

What buyers and sellers ask about Austin County.

Specifics that come up before contracts get signed.

Where is Austin County, Texas?

Directly west of Houston, inside the Texas Triangle megaregion. About an hour via I-10 to Sealy. The county seat is Bellville. Roughly an hour and a half from the city of Austin, about two hours from San Antonio.

Is Austin County the same as the city of Austin?

No. The city of Austin is the Texas state capital and sits in Travis County, about two hours northwest. Austin County is a small rural ranching county west of Houston, named for Stephen F. Austin. The two are commonly confused in searches — this page is about the rural county.

What towns are in Austin County?

Bellville (county seat), Sealy, Industry, Cat Spring, Wallis, and San Felipe are the main ones. Several smaller rural communities scatter across the county. Sealy sits on I-10. Bellville is along Highway 36.

What kind of land is for sale here?

Working cattle ranches, hay and row-crop farmland, hunting and recreational acreage, homestead-size parcels, and rural homes. Rolling, productive land with strong ag tradition.

Is ag exemption available?

Widely. Most rural acreage qualifies under cattle, hay, or wildlife management. We confirm current ag status and what it takes to keep or transfer the exemption before closing.

Do you list off-market properties in Austin County?

Often. We work directly with landowners and hear about ranches before they list. Tell us what you need by acreage range, town, or property type.
