Common Questions

Straight answers about buying Texas land.

What should I look for when buying a Texas ranch?
Water first, always, source, quality, reliability. Then access, soils, existing infrastructure, and legal encumbrances like easements and mineral rights. We run a full checklist before you tour anything.
How long does buying rural land in Texas usually take?
With clean title and straightforward financing, 30–45 days is realistic. 1031 exchanges or properties with title issues can stretch to 60–90. We map the timeline upfront so there are no surprises.
What's the difference between ag exempt and wildlife exempt?
Both dramatically reduce your annual property taxes. Ag exemption requires active agricultural use, livestock, crops, bees. Wildlife exemption requires a wildlife management plan. We explain the qualification criteria for your specific county.
Do you work with buyers doing a 1031 exchange?
Yes, and we've done exchanges ourselves. We track your 45- and 180-day deadlines, coordinate with your CPA and qualified intermediary, and move fast on pre-screened replacement properties.
Can you help if I'm new to rural land ownership?
That's most of our buyers. We slow the process down, explain everything, and connect you to the J4 Ecosystem for fencing, water, and development help. You won't be left guessing after close.
Do you have access to off-market properties?
Yes. Our network of ranch owners, landowners, and local contacts means we see deals before they're listed, sometimes before the owner even decides to sell. Tell us what you need.
